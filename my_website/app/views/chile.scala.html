Time in Chile

After graduating high school, I lived in Santiago, Chile (and neighboring areas to the west) for 2 years while serving a mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. I loved my time there, so this section is dedicated to provide some info about Chile. If you're interested in the church itself, you can learn more here.

The City

Santiago, the capital of Chile, is a massive and extremely well-developed city. I was blown away when I first saw it; being an ignorant 18-year-old, I thought Chile would be like a third-world country.

The city provides various work opportunities for Chileans as well as immigrants from neighboring countries. One of the largest buildings in South America is a business hub, and is located right in downtown Santiago.

There are also several buildings featuring architecture from the old world, many of which are popular tourist attractions. Santa Lucia (first image below) was an old war bunker.

Neighboring Areas

I also spent some time in a few cities to the west of Santiago. While the capital is industrious, these smaller towns offer amazing views of the country.

At the coast there are also cities such as San Antonio, Cartagena, and El Quisco. San Antonio is one of the biggest ports in South America.

Cartagena and El Quisco are extremely popular tourist attractions, especially in the summer. In fact, the largest swimming pool in the world can be found at a hotel in El Quisco.

The People

Chileans are very friendly and interesting people. I can honestly say that I met many people who I now consider family. They love having fun, spending time with friends and family, cooking, eating, pets, and the list goes on. For example, this guy showed his cool collections of all things action movies, including a replica of Aragorn's sword from "The Lord of the Rings".

The Food

France is typically known for producing tasty bread, but, believe it or not, Chile is a strong competitor. In fact, Chile has the second-highest bread consumption per capita in the world, I can personally witness that it is delicious. Aside from that, Chileans enjoy various dishes, including chicken, rice, beans, soups, salads, empanadas, different varieties of beef, and their own version of the hot dog: completos.

Also, one of the more popular drinks is yerba mate, a drink also common in Argentina and Uruguay (it's even better when drinking from an orange).

Conclusion

Chile is an amazing place, and I hope to go back and visit at some point. If you're looking for a new vacation spot, maybe now Chile will come to mind :)
